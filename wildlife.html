The wildlife of the Hudson Highlands

The land around the house is an oak-chestnut forest on rocky ridges, wrapped by a tidal river. That mix packs in a lot of life: wintering eagles and migrating hawks over the cliffs, black bear and bobcat in the woods, two venomous snakes on the warm ledges, and endangered sturgeon in the Hudson below.

Where to see it: the trails and the marsh are in The outdoors.
⚠ About this page. The species here were web-researched and checked against the Black Rock Forest checklists, the Constitution Marsh (Audubon) species list, New York State DEC and NOAA Fisheries — but a guide is not a field survey. What is actually out on a given day shifts with season and luck. Give wildlife room: the timber rattlesnake and copperhead are venomous (and protected — never handle or harass them), and bears are best enjoyed from a distance. Conservation-status labels reflect the time of writing and can change. Photos are from Wikimedia Commons, credited on each image; they illustrate the species and were not taken locally.
